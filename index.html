Research Project

Currently, I'm working at NCSA gravity group calculating the optimal signal to noise ratio for binary blackhole merges. Before, I was generating waveforms for binary blackhole merges.

To the left are two blackholes. You can see they bend light close to them.

This is a video about how the optimal signal to noise ratio in all direction from earth changes with respect to the polarization angle. We are using 6 detectors on earth and fixing inclination angle to π/4, The horizontal axis is longitude and the vertical axis is latitude. The color at a specific location tells you the optimal signal to noise ratio for a binary blackhole merge happening exactly above that location. The lighter the color, the higher the signal to noise ratio.

This project is in the finishing period. After this, I will probability analyze the optimal signal to noise ratio of LISA, a gravitation wave detector that will be launched into the space in the future.
